Bibimbap

A little about Bibimbap

So you've stumbled into Korean cuisine, looking for something amazing to try and now you're here!

The beloved Bibimbap (비빔밥) - full of fresh ingredients, vibrant colours and flavours to die for. Bibimbap is probably one of the most well-known Korean dishes out there. Even if you don’t know a thing about Korea, it’s not too difficult to find people who has had bibimbap sometime in their lives or even heard of it through the grapvine! If you want to explore the wonderful world of Bibimbap stay tuned and get your cooking skills ready!

Ingredients

Meat and Sauce

  • 100g / 3.5 ounces beef mince (or other cuts)
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • 1/4 tsp minced garlic

Vegetables and Others

  • 250g seasoned spinach
  • 350g seasoned bean sprouts
  • 100g shiitake mushrooms
  • 120g carrots
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 3 to 4 portions of steamed white rice
  • 3 to 4 eggs
  • Some cooking oil to cook the meat, carrots, mushrooms, eggs etc
  • Some toasted seasonsed shredded seaweed

How to Make Bibimbap (steps)

Prepare and cook ingredients as below.

  1. For meat, mix the beef mince with the meat sauce listed above. Marinate the meat for about 30 mins while you are working on other ingredients to enhance the flavour. Add some cooking oil into a wok and cook the meat on medium high to high heat. It takes about 3 to 5 mins to thoroughly cook it.
  2. Mix the bibimbap sauce ingredients in a bowl
  3. Cook beansprouts and spinach
  4. Rinse, peel and julienne the carrots. Add some cooking oil and 1/4 tsp of fine sea salt in a wok and cook the carrots on medium high to high heat for 2 to 3 mins.
  5. Clean/rinse the shiitake mushrooms and thinly slice them. Add some cooking oil and 1/4 tsp of fine sea salt in a wok and cook the mushrooms on medium high to high heat until they are all cooked. (It takes 2 to 3 mins.)
  6. Make fried eggs. (While sunny side up is common, you can make them per your preference.)
  7. Put the rice into a bowl and add the meat, assorted vegetables, seasoned seaweed, bibimbap sauce, and the egg on top of the rice.
  8. Serve! To eat, mix the ingredients in the bowl and enjoy!
